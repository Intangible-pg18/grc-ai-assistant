REGULATION: ISO/IEC 27001 Information Security Management
REGULATION ID: REG-003
JURISDICTION: International (Global Standard)
GOVERNING BODY: International Organization for Standardization (ISO) and International Electrotechnical Commission (IEC)
CURRENT VERSION: ISO/IEC 27001:2022 (Published October 2022)
PREVIOUS VERSION: ISO/IEC 27001:2013 (Superseded)
COMPANION STANDARD: ISO/IEC 27002:2022 (Implementation guidance)
APPLICABILITY TO DILIGENT: Information Security Management System (ISMS) Certification

REGULATORY OVERVIEW:
ISO 27001 is the international standard for information security management systems. It specifies requirements for establishing, implementing, maintaining, and continually improving an ISMS. ISO 27001 certification demonstrates to customers, partners, and regulators that organization has implemented internationally recognized security practices.

ISMS FRAMEWORK STRUCTURE (Clauses 4-10):

Clause 4: Context of the Organization
- 4.1: Understanding organization and its context (internal/external issues)
- 4.2: Understanding needs and expectations of interested parties (stakeholders)
- 4.3: Determining scope of ISMS (boundaries and applicability)
- 4.4: Information Security Management System (establishing and maintaining ISMS)

Clause 5: Leadership
- 5.1: Leadership and commitment (top management demonstrates commitment)
- 5.2: Policy (establishing information security policy aligned with strategic direction)
- 5.3: Organizational roles, responsibilities, authorities (assigning ISMS responsibilities)

Clause 6: Planning
- 6.1: Actions to address risks and opportunities (risk assessment and treatment)
  * 6.1.1: General planning requirements
  * 6.1.2: Information security risk assessment (identifying, analyzing, evaluating)
  * 6.1.3: Information security risk treatment (selecting and implementing controls)
- 6.2: Information security objectives and planning to achieve them (measurable objectives)
- 6.3: Planning of changes (managing ISMS changes systematically)

Clause 7: Support
- 7.1: Resources (providing necessary resources for ISMS)
- 7.2: Competence (ensuring personnel competency)
- 7.3: Awareness (ensuring awareness of information security policies and responsibilities)
- 7.4: Communication (determining internal and external communications)
- 7.5: Documented information (creating, updating, and controlling required documentation)

Clause 8: Operation
- 8.1: Operational planning and control (implementing risk treatment plans)
- 8.2: Information security risk assessment (conducting assessments per planned intervals)
- 8.3: Information security risk treatment (implementing risk treatment plans and managing residual risks)

Clause 9: Performance Evaluation
- 9.1: Monitoring, measurement, analysis, and evaluation (tracking ISMS performance)
- 9.2: Internal audit (conducting internal ISMS audits at planned intervals)
- 9.3: Management review (top management reviews ISMS suitability, adequacy, effectiveness)

Clause 10: Improvement
- 10.1: Continual improvement (improving ISMS suitability, adequacy, effectiveness)
- 10.2: Nonconformity and corrective action (addressing nonconformities with root cause analysis)

STATEMENT OF APPLICABILITY (SoA):
Document explaining which Annex A controls are:
- Applicable and implemented (with justification for how implemented)
- Applicable but not implemented (with justification for exclusion and alternative measures)
- Not applicable (with justification for non-applicability based on risk assessment)

RISK ASSESSMENT AND TREATMENT:

Risk Assessment Requirements:
- Establish and maintain information security risk criteria (risk acceptance, risk analysis)
- Identify information security risks (asset owners, threats, vulnerabilities, impacts)
- Analyze information security risks (assess likelihood and impact)
- Evaluate information security risks (compare against risk criteria, prioritize)
- Risk assessment frequency: Annually minimum, or when significant changes occur

Risk Treatment Options:
- Avoid: Eliminate risk by not starting or continuing activity
- Accept: Acknowledge risk with documented justification (requires management approval)
- Share/Transfer: Insurance, outsourcing, or contractual risk transfer
- Modify/Mitigate: Implement controls reducing likelihood or impact

Risk Treatment Plan:
Document specifying selected treatment option, controls implemented, resources required, responsibilities, and timelines.

ISO 27001 CERTIFICATION PROCESS:

Stage 1 Audit (Documentation Review):
- Auditor reviews ISMS documentation for completeness and conformity
- Assess organization's understanding of ISO 27001 requirements
- Review scope definition, risk assessment, SoA, and ISMS procedures
- Identify gaps before Stage 2 audit
- Duration: 1-3 days depending on organization size

Stage 2 Audit (Implementation Audit):
- Auditor evaluates ISMS implementation and control effectiveness
- On-site interviews with management and staff
- Evidence examination (policies, procedures, records, logs)
- Testing of controls from Annex A
- Verification of risk assessment and treatment process
- Duration: 3-7 days depending on organization size and scope

Certification Decision:
- Certification Body reviews audit findings
- Major nonconformities must be addressed before certification granted
- Minor nonconformities accepted with corrective action plan (30-90 day deadline)
- Certificate issued valid for 3 years

Surveillance Audits (Annual):
- Annual audits during 3-year certification period
- Verify ISMS maintenance and control effectiveness
- Review changes to scope, risk assessment, or control implementation
- Duration: 1-2 days
- Nonconformities must be addressed to maintain certification

Recertification Audit (Every 3 Years):
- Comprehensive audit similar to Stage 2
- Full reassessment of ISMS and Annex A controls
- Evaluates continual improvement and ISMS maturity
- Certificate renewed for another 3 years if successful

TRANSITION FROM ISO 27001:2013 TO 2022:
Organizations certified to 2013 version had until October 2025 to transition. Key changes:
- Annex A restructured from 14 domains to 4 themes (93 vs 114 controls)
- 11 new controls added (threat intelligence, cloud security, configuration management, etc.)
- Some controls merged or renumbered
- Updated to address modern threats (cloud, remote work, supply chain)

COST AND RESOURCES:
- Certification audit cost: $15,000-$50,000 (depends on organization size and scope)
- Annual surveillance: $8,000-$20,000
- Recertification: Similar to initial certification
- Internal resources: 1-2 FTE ISMS Manager, cross-functional control owners
- Preparation timeline: 6-18 months for mature organizations
