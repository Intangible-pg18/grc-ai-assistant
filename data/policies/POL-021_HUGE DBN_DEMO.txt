TITLE: Data Breach Notification and Response Policy
VERSION: 4.2
LAST UPDATED: 2025-01-15
OWNER: Chief Privacy Officer
REGULATORY MAPPING: GDPR Article 33, GDPR Article 34, CCPA Section 1798.82, HIPAA 45 CFR 164.408

PURPOSE:
This policy establishes the organization's procedures for data breach notification in compliance with GDPR Article 33 (notification to supervisory authority) and GDPR Article 34 (communication to data subjects). The policy ensures timely notification within the mandatory 72-hour timeframe specified by GDPR Article 33.

SCOPE:
Applies to all personal data breaches involving EU/EEA residents' data and all security incidents affecting California residents under CCPA.

DEFINITIONS:
- Personal Data Breach: A breach of security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to, personal data transmitted, stored or otherwise processed (GDPR Article 4(12))
- Supervisory Authority: The independent public authority responsible for monitoring GDPR compliance (in our case, the Irish Data Protection Commission)
- 72-Hour Notification Window: The mandatory timeframe from breach discovery to supervisory authority notification per GDPR Article 33(1)

NOTIFICATION REQUIREMENTS:

1. SUPERVISORY AUTHORITY NOTIFICATION (GDPR Article 33):
   Timeline: Within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach
   Required Information:
   - Nature of the personal data breach including categories and approximate number of data subjects affected
   - Name and contact details of the Data Protection Officer (DPO)
   - Description of the likely consequences of the breach
   - Measures taken or proposed to address the breach and mitigate adverse effects
   
   Exception: No notification required if breach is unlikely to result in risk to rights and freedoms of individuals

2. DATA SUBJECT NOTIFICATION (GDPR Article 34):
   Timeline: Without undue delay when breach likely to result in high risk
   Required Information:
   - Nature of the breach in clear and plain language
   - Contact point for more information (DPO)
   - Likely consequences of the breach
   - Measures taken or proposed to mitigate adverse effects
   
   Exception: Notification not required if:
   a) Technical and organizational protection measures were applied (e.g., encryption)
   b) Subsequent measures ensure high risk no longer likely to materialize
   c) It would involve disproportionate effort (public communication may substitute)

3. BREACH ASSESSMENT CRITERIA:
   High Risk Indicators requiring data subject notification:
   - Unencrypted sensitive personal data (health, biometric, financial)
   - Breach involves authentication credentials (passwords, tokens)
   - Large-scale breach affecting >1000 individuals
   - Breach involving vulnerable individuals (children, elderly)
   - Cross-border data transfer violations

NOTIFICATION PROCEDURES:

Phase 1: Initial Discovery (0-4 Hours)
- Security team identifies potential breach
- Incident Commander assigned
- DPO immediately notified
- Initial impact assessment initiated
- 72-hour countdown clock started

Phase 2: Investigation (4-48 Hours)
- Determine scope: number of affected data subjects and data categories
- Assess risk level (low, medium, high) using GDPR Article 34 criteria
- Document breach details for supervisory authority report
- Legal team reviews notification obligations
- Begin drafting supervisory authority notification

Phase 3: Supervisory Authority Notification (Within 72 Hours)
- Submit notification via Irish DPC online portal
- Include all GDPR Article 33 required elements
- If full information unavailable, provide in phases
- Document notification timestamp and confirmation receipt
- Retain all evidence and communications

Phase 4: Data Subject Notification (If Required)
- Determine notification method (email, letter, public communication)
- Draft notification in clear, plain language
- Coordinate with legal and PR teams
- Execute notification without undue delay
- Provide dedicated helpline/contact point
- Monitor and respond to data subject inquiries

DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS:
- Maintain breach register documenting all breaches (GDPR Article 33(5))
- Record: facts, effects, remedial action taken
- Retention: 5 years minimum
- Available for supervisory authority inspection

ESCALATION MATRIX:
- Critical Breach (>10,000 subjects): CEO, Board notification within 2 hours
- High Risk Breach (1,000-10,000): Executive team notification within 6 hours
- Medium Risk (100-1,000): Department heads within 12 hours
- Low Risk (<100): Standard incident process
